Our Heritage

A Legacy Woven
In Indian Tradition

Founded in 1980 in the heart of Aligarh, Hers Cottons has been a beloved destination for women who celebrate the art of Indian ethnic dressing. For over four decades, we have curated garments that honour the rich artistry of India's textile heritage — from the finest handlooms to the most intricate embroideries.

Every thread tells a story — of master kaarigars, of timeless silhouettes, and of the quiet confidence that comes from wearing something rooted in our culture. At Hers Cottons, we believe Indian ethnicity is not just clothing; it is an expression of identity.

The Beginning

Born from Aligarh's
Cultural Soul

Founder: Munir Iqbal Siddiqui

Aligarh is no ordinary city. Home to the legendary Aligarh Muslim University — one of India's most storied centres of learning — the city has long been a cradle of culture, poetry, and refinement. Here, women have walked the corridors of academia and society draped in the elegance of Indian ethnic silhouettes, their attire as much a statement of identity as of intellect.

It was in this spirit that Hers Cottons was born in 1980. The founder understood that a woman's dress carries far more than fabric — it carries her roots, her grace, and her story. She set out to create a space where the women of Aligarh could find Indian ethnic wear that honoured tradition while meeting the demands of an educated, confident, modern woman.

From salwar kameez in fine cotton to handloom sarees that whisper of handcrafted care, every piece at Hers Cottons has been chosen with the same vision: to celebrate who we are, where we come from, and the beautiful traditions that dress us.

"A woman dressed in her culture's finest is the most powerful sight in any room." — Hers Cottons, Aligarh · Est. 1980
